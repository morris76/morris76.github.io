About

Biography

Resume

Todd is a PhD candidate in sociology and longtime advocate for workers’ rights. He has worked previously as a union carpenter and as a public school teacher in New York City before returning to his home state of Connecticut to attend graduate school at UConn. He resides in Colchester with his spouse Genea, and three young children, Marley, Toliver, and Ebba, ages 9, 7, and 5.

As a sociologist, Todd’s research interests include social stratification and social movements, with a focus on the labor movement. He has worked as a UConn GA at the Connecticut Labor Education Center where he taught non-credit classes to local labor leaders and union stewards on issues such as collective bargaining, grievance handling, and arbitration. He has also taught several for-credit courses within the UConn sociology department, including Social Problems; Jobs, Work and Globalization; and Race, Class, and Gender.

Within the GEU-UAW, Todd has played an active role as an organizer since the start of the organizing effort in the Fall of 2013. He worked on collecting signatures for the recognition campaign in the Spring of 2014, lobbied state legislators to support our efforts, and helped organize each of the majority actions taken during negotiations in 2014-2015 to win our first contract. Over the summer, Todd chaired the union bylaws committee which drafted the local bylaws that govern the daily operations of our organization. Todd has also participated in the national Conference of Graduate Employee Unions in each of the past two years, where he has made presentations on democratic bargaining, successful contract campaigns, and new union organizing to members of other GA unions from across North America.
